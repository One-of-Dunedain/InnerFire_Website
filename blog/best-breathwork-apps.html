I tested these apps the way most people actually use them: quick resets during stress, short night routines before sleep, and repeated daily sessions where pricing and friction matter. Each app below includes what you really pay, what is actually free, and expanded pros/cons synthesized from user feedback patterns and billing behavior.

Apps That Actually Help With Anxiety

For anxious moments, speed beats complexity. The best options here start fast, keep cognitive load low, and do not force long setup before relief.

Breathwrk

★ 4.76 (App Store) iOS / Android Freemium App Store Google Play
Anxiety protocols

Breathwrk is still one of the fastest ways to run structured anxiety protocols. It gives clear pacing with good sensory cues, but recent ecosystem changes increased friction for some users.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $9.00/month
  • Yearly: $49.00/year in current App Store SKU list (additional active SKUs: $29.99, $38.99, $69.99).
  • Free tier: Usable, but core library depth is limited without premium.
  • Trial: No explicit free-trial duration shown in public listing; auto-renew applies to subscription plans.

Pros

  • Protocol-first design for stress states.
  • Clear inhale/exhale/hold guidance.
  • Many users report immediate calming effects when sessions run smoothly.

Cons

  • Restore/login friction reported by users.
  • Plan SKUs vary across store accounts, so renewal-price expectations can differ.
  • Android sentiment is notably weaker than iOS.
Verdict: Great if you want protocol structure for anxiety. Skip if account stability and simplicity are top priority.

Headspace

★ 4.84 (App Store) iOS / Android / Web Freemium App Store Google Play
For beginners

Headspace is still a solid beginner path because onboarding is clear and sessions are structured. The tradeoff is that breathwork is one module in a broader paid wellness product.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $12.99/month
  • Yearly: $69.99/year ($5.83/month effective)
  • Free tier: Limited intro content; serious use requires paid plan.
  • Trial: 14 days on annual plan (App Store SKU: "1 Year Plus 14 Day Free Trial"), auto-renews and card is required upfront.

Pros

  • Excellent onboarding.
  • Reliable stress/sleep guidance.
  • Large structured library helps habit consistency.

Cons

  • Less breathwork depth than specialist apps.
  • Some users report confusion around renewal and cancellation timing.
  • App performance and load complaints appear in user feedback.
Verdict: Great for beginners who want a guided framework. Skip if you only need breathing and want less subscription pressure.

Breathing Zone

★ 4.80 (App Store) iOS / Android Paid app on iOS App Store Google Play
Therapeutic pacing

Breathing Zone keeps things simple and therapeutic. It works well if you just want paced calming sessions, but some users report freezes and regressions after updates.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: None (one-time purchase model on iOS)
  • Yearly: None
  • Free tier: No free tier on iOS listing; upfront app price is $4.99 and the listing shows no in-app purchases.
  • Trial: N/A.

Pros

  • Very low cognitive load.
  • Strong long-term daily-use reports.
  • Effective therapeutic pacing for anxiety and rumination.

Cons

  • Mixed maintenance signal across platforms.
  • Functional bugs appear in recent reviews.
  • Store terms can differ by platform/region from the iOS one-time model.
Verdict: Good pick if you want straightforward therapeutic pacing. Skip if you need polished cross-platform reliability.

Breathe+

★ 4.67 (App Store) iOS Freemium App Store
iOS timer

Breathe+ is a practical iPhone timer that works well in anxious moments because setup is fast. The downside is paywall pressure: several users report that features they expected for free are now gated.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $9.99/month
  • Yearly: $29.99/year ($2.50/month effective)
  • Free tier: Limited. Basic pacing works, but premium gates many options.
  • Trial: 7 days in many flows, auto-renews, credit card required upfront.

Pros

  • Very fast to launch and run under stress.
  • Strong customization for interval breathing.
  • Users often report tangible sleep and focus benefits.

Cons

  • Frequent complaints about aggressive premium gating.
  • Value perception drops if you only need simple timers.
  • Some users report confusion around trial-to-subscription timing.
Verdict: Good timer if you want iOS-only speed and control. Skip if you want a truly free long-term option.

Othership

★ 4.91 (App Store) iOS / Android Freemium App Store Google Play
Immersive guidance

Othership is one of the most immersive guided experiences in the category, especially for energy shifts and emotional release. The main tradeoff is price: it is among the most expensive subscriptions here.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $17.99/month
  • Yearly: $129.99/year ($10.83/month effective)
  • Free tier: Limited. Enough to test tone, not enough for long-term use.
  • Trial: Free trial available (App Store SKU: "Annual Free Trial"); exact day count can vary by offer in checkout flow.

Pros

  • High-quality production and session design.
  • Strong state-shift variety (calm, focus, activation).
  • Users often report strong emotional regulation outcomes.

Cons

  • One of the highest recurring costs in this list.
  • Some users report billing and access mismatches after renewal.
  • Value can feel low if you only need simple breathing drills.
Verdict: Great fit if you want premium guided intensity and can afford it. Skip if you want a lower-cost option.

Apps That Work For Sleep

Sleep apps win when they remove friction at night. These options are the most consistent for wind-down routines, but price and free-tier depth vary a lot.

Calm

★ 4.77 (App Store) iOS / Android / Web / Apple Watch Freemium App Store Google Play
All-round sleep ecosystem

Calm still has one of the best sleep libraries in the category. It works, but most meaningful content sits behind paid plans.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $14.99/month
  • Yearly: $69.99/year ($5.83/month effective)
  • Free tier: Limited demo-level access; premium unlocks most content.
  • Trial: 7 days on annual plan (App Store SKU: "1 week free trial then annual"), auto-renews and card is required.

Pros

  • Strong sleep content production quality.
  • Wide platform coverage.
  • Large library makes it easy to find a fitting nightly routine.

Cons

  • High recurring price for many users.
  • Some users report billing and cancellation-support friction.
  • Free tier feels limited for long-term use.
Verdict: Worth it if you want a polished sleep-first ecosystem and can afford the subscription. Skip if you only need breathwork drills.

Insight Timer

★ 4.90 (App Store) iOS / Android / Web / Apple Watch Freemium App Store Google Play
Free content depth

Insight Timer gives the strongest free library in this list. You can get real value without paying, but premium upsells are frequent and the interface can feel crowded.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $9.99/month
  • Yearly: $59.99/year ($5.00/month effective)
  • Free tier: Good. Large free catalog and usable timer features.
  • Trial: 7 days on annual plan (App Store SKU: "Annual 7D Trial"), auto-renews with card required.

Pros

  • Huge free session library.
  • Strong cross-platform support.
  • Broad teacher ecosystem gives many breathing styles.

Cons

  • Premium prompts can interrupt flow.
  • Some users report unclear premium renewal or cancellation flow.
  • Interface can feel crowded for users wanting simplicity.
Verdict: Best free-value pick if you can tolerate a busier product. If you want minimal UX, choose a simpler timer app.

iBreathe

★ 4.86 (App Store) iOS / Apple Watch Free + one-time unlock App Store
Minimalist iOS timer

iBreathe is still one of the lowest-friction timer apps for daily routines. It is simple, affordable, and avoids heavy subscription pressure, but ad quality can be an issue in free mode.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: None
  • Yearly: None
  • Free tier: Good. Core breathing features are usable without paying.
  • Trial: N/A (one-time unlock model; optional purchases only).

Pros

  • Very fast setup and clear pacing UI.
  • One-time unlock instead of recurring plan.
  • High customization with low ongoing cost.

Cons

  • iOS-only product family.
  • Ad quality complaints appear in free tier.
  • Guided content depth is limited versus premium apps.
Verdict: Great daily timer for iOS users who want simplicity and low cost. Skip if you need a large guided content library.

Free And Low-Cost Breathwork Tools

If you want daily practice without subscription fatigue, this category matters most. Some apps stay useful for free, others feel free only during onboarding.

Oak - Meditation & Breathing

★ 4.84 (App Store) iOS Mostly free App Store
Truly free iOS pick

Oak is still one of the safest recommendations when someone asks for a genuinely free breathing app. It stays simple and calm, with very little monetization pressure.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: None
  • Yearly: None
  • Free tier: Good. Core breathing and meditation features are broadly usable for free.
  • Trial: N/A (no clear recurring subscription flow in current data).

Pros

  • Low-friction UI with minimal upsell noise.
  • Strong free value for basic daily routines.
  • Clear trust signal from the mostly free model.

Cons

  • iOS only.
  • Maintenance cadence concerns appear in recent reviews.
  • Smaller feature scope than large ecosystems.

Breathly

Free no-subscription app

Breathly is one of the cleanest timer-first options in the category: no account, no ads, no subscription. It is not flashy, but it is dependable for quick daily breathing resets.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: None
  • Yearly: None
  • Free tier: Good. Full app is free in current snapshots.
  • Trial: N/A.

Pros

  • Truly free and open-source model.
  • Fast setup for sleep and stress routines.
  • No account and no ad pressure.

Cons

  • Smaller review volume than mainstream apps.
  • Recent maintenance signal is weaker than top commercial apps.
  • Fewer advanced guided features.
Verdict: Strong pick if your priority is free, clean breathing practice. Skip if you want premium coaching content and polished ecosystems.

What you actually pay

Pros

  • Clear visual pacing and strong custom protocol support.

  • iPad and landscape UX limitations are reported by some users.

Prana Breath

For customization

Prana Breath is still one of the best picks for users who want full control over inhale/hold/exhale timing. It is functional and affordable, but the UX feels more utilitarian than premium competitors.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $5.99/month (App Store also lists weekly plans at $1.99-$2.99)
  • Yearly: $14.99/year ($1.25/month effective; discount SKU $9.99/year also appears)
  • Free tier: Limited. Core use is possible, advanced features are gated by Guru tier.

Pros

  • Deep interval customization.
  • Good value compared with premium subscriptions.
  • Strong Android user base with practical outcomes.

Cons

  • Less polished interface.
  • Some users report confusion around trial and first charge timing.
  • iOS social proof and ecosystem depth are smaller.

Method-specific app

Wim Hof Method is excellent if you specifically want that one protocol and its challenge structure. The value is strong for fans, but the product is naturally narrower than broader breathwork libraries.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $5.99/month
  • Yearly: $42.99/year ($3.58/month effective)
  • Free tier: Limited. Core practice exists, premium layers add depth.

Cons

  • Narrow technique scope if you want variety.

If You Hate Timers

Timer fatigue is real. If counting makes you more tense, use visual pacing tools like Breath Ball or Breathly, or switch to immersive guided formats like Othership.

For a true no-counting mode, InnerFire is currently the only app in this list where your exhale directly controls the response.

Alternatives to Calm and Headspace

If you want a similar guided feel with a different angle, Open and Stoa are the closest substitutes. Open is more premium and studio-like, while Stoa combines stoic mindset training with short breathwork sessions.

Open

★ 4.88 (App Store) iOS Freemium App Store
Premium Calm alternative

Open is polished and often feels premium from the first session, especially for guided emotional reset routines. The tradeoff is a high price and a premium-first model.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $19.99/month
  • Yearly: $149.99/year ($12.50/month effective)
  • Free tier: Limited. Designed more as preview than daily free plan.
  • Trial: Free trial available on monthly/annual plans (App Store SKUs include "Monthly Subscription Free Trial" and "Annual Subscription Free Trial"); duration can vary by offer.

Pros

  • High-production guided sessions.
  • Strong reported outcomes for stress and emotional regulation.
  • Well-suited for users who prefer polished guided flow.

Cons

  • Some users report unclear renewal and cancellation expectations.
  • Expensive compared with other guided alternatives.
  • Some users report unexpected recurring charges after trial periods.
Verdict: Strong guided product if quality is your top priority and price is secondary. Skip if you are highly price-sensitive.

Stoa

★ 4.77 (App Store) iOS / Android Freemium App Store Google Play
Philosophy + breathing blend

Stoa combines stoic lessons with breath practices, so it works best if you want mindset training plus breathing in one habit loop. It is less minimal than timer apps, but more structured than pure meditation libraries.

What you actually pay

  • Monthly: $9.99-$11.99/month
  • Yearly: $59.99-$79.99/year ($5.00-$6.67/month effective)
  • Free tier: Limited. Enough to test format, full program requires subscription.
  • Trial: 7 days in common flow, auto-renews with card required.

Pros

  • Strong daily structure and habit framing.
  • Useful for users who like philosophy-based context.
  • Combines breath practice with reflection for routine adherence.

Cons

  • Can feel pricey if you only want breathing drills.
  • Less suitable for people who want fast timer-only resets.
  • Some users report that trial-to-paid terms could be clearer.
Verdict: Good choice if you want philosophy plus breath practice in one app. Skip if you only want fast breathing timers.
